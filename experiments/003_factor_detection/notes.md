# Experiment 003: Factor Detection — Results

## Conclusion: WEAK SIGNAL / NO FACTOR DETECTION

The geometric dynamics with repulsion do NOT detect prime factor structure
beyond what log-phase proximity (magnitude) already provides.

### Key Findings

- Factor correlation (phase-only): +0.084 (noise)
- Factor correlation (full encoding): -0.166 (weak, magnitude-driven)
- Test set spread (std 49.9°) identical to random primes (std 49.8°)
- Prime power chains do NOT form phase-locked groups
- The 4↔8 locking from experiment 002 was a collapse artifact, not structure

### What Works

- Energy minimization dynamics
- Repulsion prevents collapse
- Stable equilibria form

### What Doesn't

- Factor detection beyond magnitude proximity
- Multiplicative structure emergence
- Prime power resonance

### Next Steps

If factor detection is the goal, the encoding needs rethinking.
Log-phase alone doesn't capture multiplicative relationships.
Possible directions: multiplicative phase encoding, factor-based rotations,
or explicit group-theoretic embeddings.
