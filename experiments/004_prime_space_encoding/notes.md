# Experiment 004: Prime-Space Encoding — Results

## Conclusion: ENCODING WORKS, DYNAMICS ARE ORTHOGONAL

### Key Findings

- **Static prime-space correlation: -0.910** (near-perfect factor detection)
- **Log-phase comparison: +0.118** (no detection)
- **t-test: p ≈ 0** — statistically significant factor separation
- **Prime power chains**: All 2^n identical on hypersphere (0.0° apart)
- **Mixed set**: Numbers sharing factors cluster at <0.04 rad

### What's Real

The prime exponent encoding inherently captures factor structure.
When normalized to unit vectors, numbers sharing prime bases point
in the same direction on the hypersphere. This is mathematically
guaranteed by the construction, not discovered by dynamics.

### What's Disappointing

The dynamics (attention + repulsion) DEGRADE the factor correlation
from -0.910 → +0.073. The geometric attention doesn't enhance the
structure — it mixes it.

### The Real Contribution

> Prime exponent vectors on a hypersphere create a geometric
> representation where multiplication = direction alignment.

This is a mathematical fact about the representation, not a
dynamical result. The energy-based dynamics (exp 001-002) and
the factor-aware encoding (exp 004) are independent contributions.

### Next Direction

If factor detection is the goal, the static prime-space encoding
is sufficient. The dynamics add value in different contexts:
clustering, pattern formation, energy-based optimization.
